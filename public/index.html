Personal health data bridge

Fitbit and Google Health data, moved into Apple Health.

Health Bridge is a self-hosted personal service that reads the owner's Google Health data and prepares it for import into Apple Health. It is built for private use, not as a public consumer app.

1Google account authorized by the owner through OAuth.
0Health data sold, advertised against, or shared with third parties.
24/7Small server-side bridge that caches recent samples for the owner's Apple Health import flow.

What it reads

Health Bridge requests read-only Google Health scopes for health metrics, sleep, and activity data such as HRV, resting heart rate, oxygen saturation, respiratory rate, sleep stages, steps, workouts, and heart rate.

What it does

The service normalizes Fitbit-originated Google Health records and exposes a private authenticated export endpoint that the owner's Apple Shortcut can write into Apple Health.

What it is not

Health Bridge is not affiliated with Google, Fitbit, or Apple. It is not a medical device, health-care provider, analytics broker, ad network, or public data platform.

Built for one private workflow

This site exists so the OAuth application behind Health Bridge has public app information, privacy, and terms pages. The running service is protected by private tokens and is intended for the owner's personal data portability workflow.
